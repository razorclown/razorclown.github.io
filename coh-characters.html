Nineveh Harken

The old pickup rumbled along the gravel road toward the old house. Sheba Harken sat on the porch, watching the green fireflies disappear in the headlight beams. The truck stopped, and Nineveh slid out of the cab. “Well, Ninny,” Sheba said, “how was your date? Was he nice?”

Nineveh smirked. “Why don’t you ask him?” And she walked to the rear of the truck and let down the tailgate. “Come on, now!”

With a groan, a young man shambled around the truck. The color had already faded from his skin. Nineveh pointed to the murk beyond the house. “You go lie down in the swamp with the others til I call you.”

The corpse obeyed. The green fireflies swarmed around Nineveh, drawn to the light that few could see.

Nineveh sat next to her mother, and Sheba patted her arm. “I’m sorry, baby.”

“Oh, I don’t mind! I get a new servant, and I save some other girl a world of trouble.”

Sheba kissed Nineveh on the forehead. “Well, I’m sure you’ll find a good one eventually.”

Nineveh shrugged. She really didn’t mind either way.

Rico Long

Sandoval “Big Sandy” Napolitano had survived another turf war, but just barely. Divorced twice and sporting a growing ulcer, he was more tempted than ever to leave behind the gangster life, so he decided to get some space and clear his head — in Tibet. Sandy’s time in the monastery didn’t make much of an impact, but the egg he found in a Himalayan cave did. He never wanted to be a father until the egg hatched and the tiny dragon child inside looked him in the eye. After that, Sandy was done. He raised Rico like his own son, teaching him how to survive the streets, but never returning to the mob.
